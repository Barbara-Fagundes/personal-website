Academic

Supporting students through ambiguity, not around it

I am interested in how students learn in design-centered environments, how teams navigate ambiguity, how faculty and students co-improve teaching, and how technical tools can support learning without becoming barriers to participation.

Teaching

Teaching philosophy

In first-year engineering and related learning environments, I aim to help students define problems, examine assumptions, justify decisions, and reflect on how they learn. I use structured questions, mentorship, and collaborative discussion to help learners build habits of thought they can carry forward.

Approach

What I emphasize

  • Structured inquiry in open-ended design work
  • Team collaboration as a teachable skill
  • Reflection, iteration, and evidence-based reasoning
  • Supportive learning environments with clear expectations

Research

Current academic profile

Niche

Connecting university research to educator-facing AI literacy work

A central niche in my work is the space between research and practice: how higher education institutions can help K-12 educators and students make thoughtful, informed, and ethically grounded use of AI. My background in computational thinking, early education, faculty collaboration, and AI in learning contexts makes that bridge especially compelling.
