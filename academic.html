Teaching

Supporting students through ambiguity, not around it.

In first-year engineering contexts, I see teaching as helping students build structure when there is no single obvious path forward. My role is not only to explain content, but to help learners define problems, examine assumptions, justify decisions, and revisit their thinking when new evidence emerges.

I use structured questions, reflective guidance, and collaborative discussion to help students develop habits of thought they can carry into future coursework and professional practice.

My teaching background spans first-year engineering, graduate online teaching support, K-12 curriculum development, tutoring in computer science and physics, and earlier classroom teaching in Brazil.

Research

Current research profile

My recent work includes qualitative research on undergraduate students' use of generative AI, collaborative faculty development through the Student Pedagogy Advocates program, and earlier scholarship in K-2 computational thinking, literacy integration, and engineering design.

Niche

Connecting university research to educator-facing AI literacy work.

A central niche in my work is the space between research and practice: how higher education institutions can help K-12 educators and students make thoughtful, informed, and ethically grounded use of AI. My background in computational thinking, early education, faculty collaboration, and AI in learning contexts makes that bridge especially compelling.
