Research

From AI use to learning and cognitive outcomes

My current research examines how undergraduate students use generative AI in engineering and computer science courses, with attention to reasoning, decision-making, help-seeking, and patterns of reliance on AI-generated outputs.

I am especially interested in questions about autonomy, deeper learning, transfer, productive support, and the conditions under which AI helps or hinders the development of capability.

That work also connects to a broader education question I care about: how universities can help K-12 systems build AI literacy in ways that are technically informed, educationally meaningful, and ethically responsible.
